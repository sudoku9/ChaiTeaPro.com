How to make starbucks chai tea latte?

ByChaiTea May 20, 2023May 20, 2023 Homemade

Starbucks offers Teavana brand Chai Tea bags in their stores and online. These tea bags are made with a blend of black tea, ginger, cinnamon, cardamom, and vanilla flavors, creating a spicy and aromatic chai flavor. They can be brewed at home by simply adding the tea bag to hot water and steeping it for a few minutes. You can also use them to make a Chai Tea Latte by steeping the tea bag in hot water and then adding steamed milk and sweetener of your choice. You can purchase these tea bags on Starbucks’ website or in their stores.

  • 2 tablespoons honey or sugar (optional)

Instructions:

  • In a small saucepan, bring water to a boil.
  • Remove from heat and add the tea bags. Steep the tea bags for 5 minutes.
  • Remove the tea bags from the saucepan and stir in honey or sugar (if using).
  • Add the milk to the saucepan and bring the mixture to a simmer. Simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  • Strain the tea mixture into a cup and use a milk frother to create foam on the milk. Alternatively, you can stir the milk vigorously with a whisk until frothy.
  • Pour the milk mixture over the tea and enjoy.
  • That’s it! You can customize the sweetness and milk type to your liking.

Here is a recipe for making a Starbucks Iced Chai Tea Latte at home:

Ingredients:

  • 2 Chai tea bags
  • ¬æ cup filtered water
  • ¬º cup whole milk (or non-dairy milk like almond or coconut)
  • 2 tablespoons honey or sugar (optional)
  • Ice cubes

Instructions:

  • In a small saucepan, bring water to a boil.
  • Remove from heat and add the tea bags. Steep the tea bags for 5 minutes.
  • Remove the tea bags from the saucepan and stir in honey or sugar (if using).
  • Add the milk to the saucepan and bring the mixture to a simmer. Simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  • Let the tea mixture cool down to room temperature.
  • Fill a glass with ice cubes.
  • Pour the tea mixture into the glass over the ice.
  • Add additional milk if desired.
  • Stir and enjoy!

That’s it! You can adjust the sweetness and milk type to your preference to make it the perfect iced chai latte for you.

üíÅ‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è What chai tea does starbucks use?

Starbucks uses a proprietary blend of chai tea for its chai tea lattes. However, the company has revealed that the blend contains black tea, ginger, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, and black pepper. Starbucks’ chai tea concentrate is made using this blend of ingredients, as well as sugar and honey, and it’s heated with milk to make a chai tea latte. While the chai concentrate is not available for purchase, you can use chai tea bags or loose-leaf chai tea to make a similar drink at home.

üíÅ‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è How many calories in a starbucks chai tea latte?

The number of calories in a Starbucks chai tea latte varies depending on the size of the drink and the type of milk used. Here is a breakdown of the approximate calories for different sizes and milk options:

  • Tall (12 oz) with nonfat milk: 140 calories
  • Grande (16 oz) with nonfat milk: 190 calories
  • Venti (20 oz) with nonfat milk: 240 calories
  • Tall (12 oz) with whole milk: 170 calories
  • Grande (16 oz) with whole milk: 240 calories
  • Venti (20 oz) with whole milk: 310 calories

It’s important to note that if you add any additional sweeteners or flavors, it will increase the calorie count. Additionally, if you opt for alternative milk options like almond or coconut milk, the calorie count may vary.

üíÅ‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è What is in a starbucks chai tea latte

A Starbucks chai tea latte typically contains:

  • Chai tea concentrate made from a blend of black tea, cardamom, cinnamon, black pepper, ginger, and cloves
  • Milk (whole, 2%, nonfat, almond, or coconut milk)
  • Water
  • Optional sweetener (such as honey, sugar, or classic syrup)

The ingredients may vary slightly depending on the size and customization of the drink. The tea concentrate is made in large batches and stored in containers at Starbucks locations, so the specific recipe may differ from what you can make at home.

Yes, Starbucks chai tea lattes contain caffeine. The amount of caffeine varies depending on the size of the drink and other factors like the type of tea and strength of the concentrate. This amount is less than a cup of coffee, but it can still affect some people who are sensitive to caffeine.
